Customer Churn & Revenue Risk Analysis (Power BI Project)
Project Overview

This project analyzes customer churn behavior, revenue loss, and retention risk using a Telco customer dataset through Power BI.

The goal was to identify:

Which customer segments are most likely to churn
How churn impacts total revenue
Which contract types and payment methods carry the highest risk
How tenure, age, and monthly charges influence customer retention

This dashboard was designed to simulate a real business scenario where reducing churn and protecting revenue are key priorities.

Tools Used
Power BI
Excel (Data Cleaning & Preparation)
DAX (Calculated Columns, Measures, KPI Development)
Key Business Questions
1. Which contract type has the highest churn risk?
2. How much revenue is lost due to churn?
3. Which customer age groups are most vulnerable?
4. Does shorter tenure increase churn probability?
5. Which payment methods are associated with higher churn?

Dashboard Structure
Page 1: Executive Summary

Provides a high-level overview of:
Total Customers: 100K
Total Revenue: $292.6M
Churn Rate: 33.14%
Revenue Lost: $99.67M
Main Insight:

Month-to-month customers represent the highest churn segment and contribute the largest revenue loss.

Page 2: Customer Behavior

Focuses on behavioral and demographic churn patterns:

Churn by Age Group
Churn by Tenure Group
Monthly Charges by Churn
Churn by Payment Method
Main Insights:
Senior customers showed the highest churn count
Newer customers (0–12 months tenure) were among the highest-risk groups
Electronic check users had the highest churn
Customers with higher monthly charges showed elevated churn behavior
Page 3: Revenue Impact

Examines financial consequences:

Revenue Lost by Contract, Payment Method, and Age Group
Total Revenue by Tenure
Main Insight:

Revenue loss is heavily concentrated among month-to-month customers, especially across specific payment methods.

Key Findings
Customer Retention Risks:
Month-to-month contracts are the most vulnerable
Shorter tenure strongly correlates with churn
Electronic check customers are higher risk
Revenue exposure from churn is significant (34.06%)
Strategic Recommendations
1. Encourage long-term contracts
Offer incentives for one-year or two-year transitions
2. Improve early-stage retention
Develop onboarding or loyalty programs for new customers
3. Review payment method friction
Investigate why electronic check customers churn more frequently
4. Focus on high-risk segments
Target vulnerable age and tenure groups with retention campaigns.
